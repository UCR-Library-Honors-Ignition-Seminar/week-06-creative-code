# In-Class Activity: Bitsy

---

## Part 1: Play and Observe (~15 min)

Play **one** of the following before opening the editor:

- [*Endless Scroll*](https://haraiva.itch.io/endless-scroll) by Cecile Richard
- [*Hawk & Puma*](https://nieblagames.itch.io/hawk-and-puma) by Nico Valdivia Hennig (UCR student)
- Browse the [Bitsy Essay Jam entries](https://itch.io/jam/the-bitsy-essay-jam/entries) and pick one that catches your eye

As you play, notice:
- Where does the meaning come from — the pixels, the movement, or the words?
- What can the maker *not* show in this format? How do they work around it?
- Does this feel like a game, an essay, a poem — or something else?

**Discuss as a class:**
- Could the work you played have been a written essay instead? What would be different?
- The pixel grid is extremely limited — 8×8 tiles, two colors per room, no sound. Did you feel that limitation while playing, or did it disappear?
- What does Bitsy let a maker do that a written paragraph cannot?

---

## Part 2: Build a 2-Room Journey (~25 min)

Open [bitsy.org](https://bitsy.org) — no account needed.

Your goal: make something with **2 rooms** that conveys **one feeling, memory, or question**. Not a full story — just a single idea that changes between the two rooms. Some starting points:

- A room that feels crowded → a room that feels empty
- A familiar place → the same place at a different time
- A question in Room 1 → no answer in Room 2 (or a different question)

**Minimum to aim for:**
- [ ] An avatar you drew yourself
- [ ] 2 rooms with different tile arrangements — the visual difference between them should mean something
- [ ] At least one sprite with a line of dialogue
- [ ] An exit connecting the rooms

**If you finish early:**
- Add a second sprite in Room 2 that responds differently than the one in Room 1
- Add an ending — what happens when the player reaches the final space?
- Change the room colors — what does each color suggest?

---

## Part 3: Discussion (~15 min)

If you would like to share your game with the class, you are welcome to — just play it on the projector, no explanation needed first. Sharing is optional.

Whether or not you share, discuss as a class:

- What was the hardest design decision you made? Not the most technically difficult — the most *meaningful* one.
- Did the pixel grid or the two-color limit push your idea in a direction you didn't expect?
- What did you want to express that the tool wouldn't let you — and did you find a workaround, or did you change the idea?

**Closing question:**
Bitsy games are often described as "small worlds." What makes a world feel complete even when it is tiny — is it how much is in it, or how intentional each element is?
